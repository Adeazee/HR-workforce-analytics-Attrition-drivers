
# 📊 HR Workforce Analytics: Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition significantly impacts organizational productivity, morale, and recruitment costs.  

This project analyzes an HR employee dataset using **Power BI** to identify key drivers of attrition and provide data-driven recommendations to improve employee retention.

The objective is to uncover patterns behind workforce turnover and support strategic HR decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

High employee turnover leads to:

- Increased recruitment and training costs  
- Loss of organizational knowledge  
- Reduced team productivity  
- Lower employee morale  

This analysis aims to answer:

- What factors drive employee attrition?
- Does overtime increase turnover risk?
- Does compensation affect retention?
- How does job satisfaction influence attrition?
- Are early-career employees more likely to leave?


---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies Used

- Power BI  
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)  
- Data Modeling  
- Data Transformation  
- Interactive Dashboard Design  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

Employees working overtime showed significantly higher attrition rates.

Lower-income employees were more likely to leave.

Attrition was highest among employees with short tenure (0–2 years).

Poor job satisfaction and work-life balance were strongly linked to turnover.

Certain departments experienced higher turnover compared to others.

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations

Implement workload monitoring to reduce burnout.

Review compensation strategy for lower-income roles.

Strengthen onboarding and mentorship programs.

Create clear career progression pathways.

Develop department-specific retention strategies.
